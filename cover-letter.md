# Skill: Cover Letter Writer

## What this does
Writes a cover letter that sounds like a real human wrote it — not a template.
Short, specific, confident. Never generic. Never sycophantic.

## Instructions for Claude

1. Read `resumes/my-resume.md`
2. Read `job-descriptions/target-role.md`
3. Identify:
   - The single most compelling reason this person is right for this role
   - One specific achievement from their background that directly maps to the role's needs
   - One genuine reason they'd want this specific role/company (infer from the JD)
4. Write a cover letter that:
   - Opens with a hook — not "I am writing to apply for..."
   - Is 3 paragraphs max, under 300 words
   - Paragraph 1: why you, for this specific role
   - Paragraph 2: one concrete proof point from your experience
   - Paragraph 3: why this company/role, and clear ask
   - Closes confidently — not "I hope to hear from you"
5. Save to `outputs/cover-letter.md`

## What to avoid
- "I am passionate about..."
- "I have always been interested in..."
- Repeating the resume
- Mentioning every job they've ever had
- Ending with weakness ("I hope", "if possible", "I would love the opportunity")

## Tone
Direct. Warm. Specific. Like a confident person talking — not a form letter.

## Output format
The complete cover letter ready to send.
Followed by one line: the single strongest thing about this letter.

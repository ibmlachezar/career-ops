# Skill: Outreach Writer

## What this does
Writes a cold outreach message to a hiring manager or recruiter.
Short, specific, not desperate. Gets replies.

## Instructions for Claude

1. Read `resumes/my-resume.md`
2. Read `job-descriptions/target-role.md`
3. Write TWO versions of the outreach:

### Version A: LinkedIn message (300 characters max)
Ultra short. Just enough to get a response.
- One sentence on who you are
- One sentence on why this specific role/company
- Clear ask (15-min call / happy to share resume)

### Version B: Email outreach (150 words max)
- Subject line that doesn't sound like spam
- Opening: one specific thing about the company or role that's genuine
- Middle: one line on your most relevant experience
- Ask: specific, low-friction (15-min call, not "please consider me")
- Closing: confident, not needy

4. Save both to `outputs/outreach.md`

## Rules
- Never start with "I hope this message finds you well"
- Never say "I came across your posting"
- Never list all your experience — one thing only
- Never sound desperate
- Specific > generic every time

## Output format
```
VERSION A — LINKEDIN (for direct messages):
[message]

VERSION B — EMAIL:
Subject: [subject line]
[email body]

NOTES:
[one tip on when/how to send this]
```
